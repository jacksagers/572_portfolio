Tools utilized: Mapbox Studio, Adobe Illustrator

Created: October 2021

Client/Audience: Graphic Design in Cartography course at UW–Madison (Geography: 572)


This global tileset was created as a project in UW-Madison’s Graphic Design in Cartography course and was built up from a blank template in Mapbox Studio and includes eight (8) self-designed. The tileset is inspired by the iconic black and white film photography of Ansel Adams, specifically his photographs from around the National Parks and Public Lands of the western United States. I picked Adams’ photography as my inspiration because I like many have been entranced by his photography, but I also liked the idea and challenge of constraining my work around a limiting monochromatic palette.
